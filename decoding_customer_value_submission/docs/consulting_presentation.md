## Slide 2: The Core Strategic Question
### The Promo Dilemma: Margin Cost or Loyalty Driver?

* **Visual Layout & Recommendations**:
  * **Theme**: Dual-column contrast card layout.
  * **Left Card (Muted Slate Background)**: **Promo Buyers** (43% of base / 1,677 customers).
  * **Right Card (Emerald Green Border)**: **Full-Price Buyers** (57% of base / 2,223 customers).
  * **Center Callout**: A visual formula comparing average tenure (purchases).

* **Slide Content**:
  * **The Core Hypothesis**: "Do promotions drive incremental customer tenure (repeat purchase counts)?"
  * **The Empirical Verdict**: **Hypothesis Confirmed (✅)** — Promo and full-price buyers have near-identical tenure.
  * **Key Cohort Metrics**:
    * **Promo Buyers**: 25.74 average previous purchases | $59.28 average ticket size.
    * **Full-Price Buyers**: 25.06 average previous purchases | $60.13 average ticket size.
    * **The Margin Variance**: The difference in lifetime tenure is a negligible **0.68 purchases** (a 2.7% variance), while average transaction spend remains virtually identical.
  * **Strategic Recommendation**: Immediate intervention is required. The current promotional program does not incentivize brand loyalty; instead, it subsidizes organic demand.

* **Speaker Notes**:
  * "To answer the brand's core question, we set up a hypothesis test: do customers who receive discounts buy more frequently or stay with us longer than those who buy at full price?"
  * "The data provides a very clear, empirical answer: No. Promo buyers have an average tenure of 25.74 purchases, while full-price buyers have an average tenure of 25.06 purchases. That difference is less than three-quarters of a single order over the customer's lifetime."
  * "Furthermore, the average transaction size for both groups is practically identical, hovering around $60. This confirms that our promotions are not driving larger baskets or longer retention. They represent direct margin erosion on repeat purchases that customers would have made anyway. This finding is the commercial justification for a complete restructure of our loyalty and promotional framework."

## Slide 3: Scoring Methodology: Framework A vs. B
### Redefining Loyalty: Moving Beyond Volume to Margin Health

* **Visual Layout & Recommendations**:
  * **Theme**: Horizontal process flowchart mapping the transition from Framework A to B.
  * **Left Side**: Framework A (Tenure-Only) single input.
  * **Right Side**: Framework B (Composite) three-signal weighted formula.
  * **Bottom Callout**: Methodology note detailing the rounding and sensitivity validation results.

* **Slide Content**:
  * **Framework A (Baseline)**: Bins customers solely by purchase count.
    * *Formula*: `Score A = Previous Purchases / 50` (Percentile Rank).
    * *Limitation*: Treats a 45-purchase discount buyer identically to a 45-purchase full-price buyer.
  * **Framework B (Primary Composite)**: Evaluates tenure, commitment, and margin.
    * *Formula*: `Score B = (Tenure Score × 0.50) + (Subscription Flag × 0.30) + (No-Discount Flag × 0.20) × 100`
  * **Weight Sensitivity Validation**:
    * Baseline weights (50/30/20) are highly intentional strategic parameters.
    * Sensitivity testing shows moderate responsiveness to alternate weightings, with tier shifts of **9.1%** under a tenure-heavy model (60/25/15) and **14.6%** under a subscription-heavy model (40/35/25).
  * **Methodology Note**: The pipeline rounds scores to 2 decimal places before tiering, while sensitivity tests evaluate raw floats, causing a minor baseline discrepancy of **0.44%** (immaterial to strategic decisions).

* **Speaker Notes**:
  * "Our next step was to design a segmentation framework that reflects this reality. Standard loyalty frameworks rely only on tenure—what we call Framework A."
  * "Under Framework A, a customer with 40 purchases who only buys on promo is ranked in the top tier, right next to a customer with 40 purchases who pays full price. This is commercially dangerous because it obscures our true margin drivers."
  * "We developed Framework B, a composite scoring system. It allocates 50% weight to tenure, 30% to voluntary subscription commitment, and 20% to discount independence."
  * "To ensure this scoring is statistically sound, we ran a sensitivity analysis. If we change the weights to favor tenure heavily, 9.1% of customers shift tiers. If we shift to a subscription-heavy model, 14.6% of customers shift. This moderate responsiveness confirms that our chosen weights are active, defensible strategic decisions rather than interchangeable numbers."
  * "A minor rounding difference of 0.44% of customers exists between the raw sensitivity floats and the rounded integers in the database, which has zero material impact on our strategic findings."

## Slide 5: The Hidden Analytical Skew
### Navigating Database Bias: The Gender & Subscription Anomaly

* **Visual Layout & Recommendations**:
  * **Theme**: Dual-card diagnostic display with high-contrast warning highlights (Amber/Red).
  * **Left Side**: Male customer cohort analysis.
  * **Right Side**: Female customer cohort analysis.
  * **Bottom Callout**: Visual diagram showing how the 30% subscription weight impacts female score caps.

* **Slide Content**:
  * **The Discovery**: A structural anomaly in the database shows 100% gender segmentation on programs.
  * **Male Cohort (68% of base / 2,652 customers)**:
    * **39.7%** Subscription Rate | **63.2%** Promotional Rate.
    * Males make up 84.5% of the Champion segment due to subscription access.
  * **Female Cohort (32% of base / 1,248 customers)**:
    * **0%** Subscription Rate | **0%** Promotional Rate (100% full-price organic).
  * **The Loyalty Score Impact**:
    * Under Framework B, Females are locked out of subscription points (30% weight) but score 100% on discount independence (20% weight).
    * Their maximum possible loyalty score is capped at **70.0**.
    * Consequently, Females are structurally underrepresented as Champions (11.3% of Females vs. 17.4% of Males) despite being our **healthiest, full-price margin segment**.

* **Speaker Notes**:
  * "During our validation audit, we uncovered a critical structural anomaly in the dataset that has massive implications for our marketing strategy."
  * "100% of our female customers—1,248 individuals—have a 0% subscription rate and a 0% promotion rate. They purchase exclusively at full price. Conversely, our active subscribers and promo-dependent cohorts are entirely male."
  * "Under Framework B, because Females cannot access the subscription program, they miss out on that 30% scoring weight. Even if a female customer has a perfect tenure score of 50, her maximum possible score is capped at 70."
  * "This means Females are structurally underrepresented in our Champion segment, even though they are our most margin-healthy cohort—buying entirely organically at full price. We must adjust our Power BI filters and establish a separate loyalty track for Females to protect this margin-critical group."

## Slide 7: Restructuring Promotions: The Sunset Pool
### Capturing Immediate Margin Value from High-Volume Buyers

* **Visual Layout & Recommendations**:
  * **Theme**: Financial card design with high-contrast orange/gold borders.
  * **KPI Display**:
    * **198** Target Customers
    * **$11.6K** Transaction Value Shifted to Full Price
    * **$2.3K** Est. Margin Recovered Per Order Cycle (assuming 20% discount)

* **Slide Content**:
  * **Target Segment**: 198 high-tenure, promo-reliant, non-subscribed customers (all Male).
  * **The Mathematical Anomaly**:
    * With `subscription_flag = 0` and `discount_dependent = 1`, their composite Score B is mathematically identical to their raw purchase count (`Score B = previous_purchases`).
    * Despite having high tenure (avg 43.16 purchases), their score is capped at 50, locking them in the **Growth** tier with **0% confirmed high-value status**.
  * **Strategic Action Plan (90-Day Rollout)**:
    * **Days 1–30**: Pivot email marketing away from discount codes to product-focused messaging.
    * **Days 31–60**: Restrict generic code entry. Introduce a maximum discount cap (e.g., $10 off) or raise the minimum order value to $80 (average transaction size is currently $58.58).
    * **Days 61–90**: Complete sunset of promos. Swap discounts for Free Express Shipping.

* **Speaker Notes**:
  * "Let's talk about our immediate financial opportunity: the 198 promo sunset candidates."
  * "These are customers who are highly active, averaging over 43 purchases, but they buy exclusively using promotions and have not subscribed. Because of this, their loyalty score is capped at 50, locking them in the Growth tier despite their high volume."
  * "By sunsetting promotions for this specific group, we shift $11.6K of gross transaction value to full price. Assuming a standard 20% average discount, this recovers approximately $2.3K in direct margin per order cycle."
  * "We will roll this out over 90 days. First, by shifting their email communications to focus on new arrivals rather than coupons. Next, by raising the minimum spend to qualify for discounts to $80—which is higher than their average transaction size of $58.58. Finally, we remove discounts entirely and replace them with free express shipping, which holds high perceived value but costs us far less than a 20% margin cut."

## Slide 8: The Subscription Conversion Pipeline
### Converting Mid-Tenure Promo Buyers to Secure Lifetime Value

* **Visual Layout & Recommendations**:
  * **Theme**: Horizontal conversion pipeline funnel showing three tenure bands of promo-reliant, non-subscribed customers (total 624).
  * **Funnel Stages**:
    * **High Tenure (198)**: Sunset candidates (already addressed).
    * **Mid Tenure (246)**: Upsell conversion targets (Cyber Blue highlight).
    * **Low Tenure (180)**: Lifecycle monitoring segment (Muted Slate).

* **Slide Content**:
  * **The Target Cohort**: 246 mid-tenure promo-reliant, non-subscribed customers (avg 24.02 purchases, avg spend $59.26).
  * **Strategic Objective**: Convert to active subscription *before* removing promotion access.
  * **The Subscription Upsell Mechanic**:
    * Pitch a dedicated subscriber campaign: *"Lock in a 10% discount on every order with a subscription."*
    * This aligns directly with their discount-centric behavior while locking in long-term transactional frequency.
  * **The Loyalty Impact**:
    * Activating a subscription adds **30 points** to their composite score under Framework B.
    * This raises their average score from 24 to 54, positioning them for transition from **Growth** to **Champion** once tenure increases, securing recurring margin quality.

* **Speaker Notes**:
  * "While we sunset promotions for the high-tenure group, we must take a different approach with the 246 mid-tenure promo buyers."
  * "These customers average 24 purchases. They are highly active but rely on discounts. If we cut off their promotions immediately, we risk losing them before they are fully locked in."
  * "Instead, we will upsell them to a subscription. In our database, 100% of active subscribers use discounts, so this matches their discount-seeking behavior perfectly. We offer them a guaranteed, stable subscriber discount of 10% in exchange for subscribing."
  * "This does two things: it secures their volume, and it adds 30 points to their composite loyalty score. This elevates them in our value framework and positions them to transition into Champions as their tenure grows, turning transactional buyers into stable, subscribed advocates."
